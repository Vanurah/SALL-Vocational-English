Main Activity · Grammar

Grammar Tasks

Grammar here isn't abstract — it's the language pattern behind real workplace situations: introducing yourself, following instructions, asking questions, and talking about problems politely.

↓ Stage 1: Beginner ↓ Stage 2: Intermediate ↓ Stage 3: Advanced
Stage 1 · Beginner Simple Present & introducing yourself

Goal: introduce yourself and describe daily routines and facts at work.

Rule: Use the Simple Present for facts, routines, and your job role.
Subject + verb (+s/es for he/she/it) + object.
I work in the kitchen. → She works in the kitchen.
My name is Dani. I am a student at SMK Bina Karya.
We start work at 7 AM and finish at 3 PM.

Worksheet — fill in the blank (write your answers in your notebook or the Reflection Log):

  1. My name ____ (be) Rani. I ____ (study) Hospitality at SMK Nusantara.
  2. Every morning, I ____ (check) the equipment before my shift ____ (start).
  3. My supervisor ____ (give) instructions, and I ____ (follow) them carefully.

Check your answers and get instant feedback on the Self-Check page.

Stage 2 · Intermediate Imperatives, modals & instructions

Goal: give and understand instructions, talk about rules, ability, and obligation.

Rule: Use the imperative (base verb) to give instructions.
Use must / should / have to to talk about rules and obligations.
Turn off the machine before cleaning it.
You must wear safety gloves in this area.
You should ask your supervisor if you are unsure.
Employees have to clock in before 8 AM.

Worksheet — choose the correct modal:

  1. You ____ (must / can) wear a helmet on the construction site — it's a safety rule.
  2. ____ (Should / Must) I report this to my supervisor, or can I fix it myself?
  3. Staff ____ (have to / could) submit the report by 5 PM every Friday.

Try the matching quiz on the Self-Check page for instant feedback.

Stage 3 · Advanced Polite requests, passive voice & reported problems

Goal: communicate politely and professionally, describe processes objectively, and report issues clearly.

Rule: Use polite question forms (Could you / Would you mind) for requests, and the passive voice when the process matters more than who does it.
Could you check this order before it ships?
Would you mind explaining the procedure again?
The machine is inspected every morning. (passive — focus on the process)
The order was delayed because of a supplier issue. (reporting a problem)

Worksheet — rewrite each sentence as instructed:

  1. Make this polite: "Check the invoice again." → Could you ______?
  2. Change to passive voice: "The technician inspects the machine daily." → The machine ______.
  3. Report this problem politely to a manager: "The delivery is late and it's not my fault."

Compare your answers with feedback on the Self-Check page, or send your written answer to a teacher on the Feedback page for personal comments.
