Main Activity · Vocabulary

Vocabulary Builder

Words you'll actually use at work — greeting customers, following safety rules, talking about tasks and tools. Pick the stage that matches your level. It's fine to start at Stage 1 even if you've studied English before; this vocabulary is workplace-specific.

↓ Stage 1: Beginner ↓ Stage 2: Intermediate ↓ Stage 3: Advanced
Stage 1 · Beginner Everyday workplace words

Goal: recognize and use basic words for people, places, and objects at work.

colleague n.
a person you work with
"My colleague helped me carry the boxes."
supervisor n.
the person who checks and guides your work
"Ask your supervisor if you're not sure."
shift n.
a fixed period of working time
"My shift starts at 8 AM."
uniform n.
special clothes worn for work
"Please wear your uniform every day."
customer n.
a person who buys something or uses a service
"Always smile at the customer."
tool n.
equipment used to do a job
"Put the tool back after you finish."
break n.
a short rest during work
"We have a 30-minute break at noon."
schedule n.
a plan of times for work or tasks
"Check the schedule for tomorrow's shift."

✏️ Practice: Write one sentence using three of these words about your own school workshop or internship place. Save it in your Reflection Log.

Stage 2 · Intermediate Tasks, instructions & processes

Goal: describe tasks, give and follow instructions, talk about quality and safety.

procedure n.
a fixed series of steps for doing something correctly
"Follow the safety procedure before starting the machine."
malfunction v./n.
to stop working correctly; a fault
"The printer malfunctioned during the shift."
inspect v.
to check something carefully
"Inspect the equipment before you use it."
deadline n.
the time by which something must be finished
"We have to finish the order before the deadline."
complaint n.
a statement that something is wrong or unsatisfactory
"Handle every customer complaint politely."
maintenance n.
regular work to keep equipment in good condition
"Maintenance is done every Friday."
defective adj.
having a fault; not working properly
"Remove any defective items from the line."
briefing n.
a short meeting giving instructions or information
"The morning briefing covers today's targets."

✏️ Practice: Describe one procedure from your major (e.g., starting a machine, preparing a dish, checking-in a guest) using at least four of these words.

Stage 3 · Advanced Professional & technical communication

Goal: discuss problems, give recommendations, and communicate professionally in more complex situations.

accountable adj.
responsible and required to explain actions
"Each technician is accountable for their own checklist."
discrepancy n.
a difference between two things that should match
"There's a discrepancy between the order and the invoice."
escalate v.
to pass a problem to someone with more authority
"Escalate the issue to the supervisor if it isn't resolved."
compliance n.
acting according to rules, laws, or standards
"Compliance with safety standards is checked monthly."
feasible adj.
possible to do successfully
"Is it feasible to finish the project by Friday?"
liaise v.
to communicate and cooperate with another team or person
"You'll need to liaise with the kitchen team about timing."
contingency n.
a backup plan for unexpected events
"We need a contingency in case the delivery is late."
streamline v.
to make a process simpler and more efficient
"We streamlined the check-in process to save time."

✏️ Practice: Write a short message to a supervisor reporting a problem and suggesting a solution, using at least four of these words.
